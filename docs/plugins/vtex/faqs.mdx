### How does Yuno handle expired Pix payments?
Yuno automatically tracks the expiration of every Pix payment based on the expiration date configured in your Yuno integration. Before a Pix payment expires, Yuno proactively checks whether it has been completed. If the shopper does not complete the payment in time, Yuno notifies VTEX, which automatically cancels the order. You do not need to configure or monitor anything for this to work.

### How are saved cards and Click to Pay handled?
Saved-card flows and Click to Pay both rely on a customer record being created in Yuno for each shopper. To enable them, set the **Create Customer** field to **Yes** when configuring Yuno as a payment provider. With this option enabled, the plugin creates (or updates) a Yuno customer record on every payment, which unlocks features that depend on customer identity — including saved cards and Click to Pay.

If **Create Customer** is set to **No**, every payment is processed as a one-time transaction and these features are not available.

### How do I switch between sandbox and production?
The Yuno environment your store connects to is determined by the prefix of your Yuno Public API Key:
* `dev_*`, `staging_*`, and `sandbox_*` prefixes connect to the corresponding Yuno test environments.
* `prod_*` prefixes connect to the production environment.

To switch a store from testing to production, replace the **Public API Key** (and the matching **Private Secret Key**) in your Yuno provider configuration in the VTEX dashboard with the production key pair from your Yuno dashboard.

### How can I customize what appears on my shoppers' card statements?
Use the **Soft Descriptor** field when configuring Yuno as a payment provider. The text you enter in that field is sent to the acquirer and shown on the shopper's card statement next to the charge. We recommend a short, recognizable name (for example, your store name) to help shoppers identify the purchase and reduce chargebacks.

### My business runs multiple VTEX accounts. Do I need a separate Yuno integration for each?
Not necessarily. If all your VTEX accounts share the same Yuno credentials — for example, a franchise model with one main account and several satellite accounts — you can use the **Main Account** fields when configuring Yuno as a provider. 

If each VTEX account uses its own Yuno credentials, configure Yuno as a provider in each VTEX account independently, using each account's own Yuno keys.

### I have several Yuno accounts under a single VTEX store. Can I use them all?
Yes. A single VTEX account can be connected to multiple Yuno integrations when you need to process different payment methods through different Yuno accounts. Configure Yuno as a payment provider once for each integration, giving each one a distinct **Affiliation Name**. Then, when activating payment methods, use the **Process with affiliation** field to choose which Yuno integration should process each method.

### Where can I troubleshoot a specific order?
Open the order in your **Yuno dashboard** to see the full lifecycle of every payment attempt — including provider responses, status changes, and any errors. Cross-reference the VTEX order ID with the corresponding Yuno payment to follow the transaction end-to-end.
